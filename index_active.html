Our Mission

Workforce reality,
measured.

Everyone talks about burnout. Nobody measures it.

Hospitals run their own surveys and bury the results. Recruiters tell new grads what they want to hear. Nothing captures what working in healthcare actually looks like from the inside — so nothing changes.

ROSC is a clinician-built data platform. We collect anonymous, standardized data on burnout, staffing, leadership, and retention, and turn it into a score the field can't ignore. Built by clinicians, owned by clinicians, used to make the next job better than the last.

Before you begin
Please read & agree to participate

Who we are

ROSC is an independent workforce data project, operated by two practicing clinicians acting in their personal capacity — not on behalf of any hospital, employer, professional society, or academic institution. This survey is not academic research and is not conducted under an Institutional Review Board.

What we collect

Your survey responses, plus your role, specialty, hospital/system, facility type, years in role, and U.S. region. We collect no name, no email, no phone number, and no IP address tied to your response.

How we use it

Your response is combined with many others to calculate a personal score, build aggregated benchmarks, and publish aggregated, deidentified findings. We apply a minimum group size of 10 responses before any comparison group is shown publicly.

What we will not do

We will not sell your individual response, share it with your employer or any third party, or attempt to identify you. No hospital-specific result is published unless that group has at least 10 responses.

Voluntary & anonymous

Participation is completely voluntary, with no compensation and no penalty for declining. Because the survey is fully anonymous, once you submit, your response cannot be withdrawn — we cannot tell which response is yours.

Risks

Some questions ask about burnout and intent to leave; reflecting on these may bring up uncomfortable feelings. If you are in distress, please consider contacting the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988 in the U.S.). Do not include identifying details about yourself, patients, or colleagues in open-ended fields.

Eligibility

By participating, you confirm you are 18 or older and currently work, or have worked within the past 12 months, in a U.S. healthcare setting.

Questions

The people behind ROSC

Why we built this.

We met in nursing school in 2016, connected by a shared drive and a vision that complacency was never part of the plan.

After graduation, we took different paths. One into the ER, one into the ICU. One became a CRNA, the other a nurse practitioner. We started families, bought homes, worked the call shifts, carried the weight.

And here's what we kept coming back to: everyone talks about it. Nobody measures it. Hospitals filter their internal surveys. Recruiters tell new grads what they want to hear. Nothing captures what working in healthcare looks like from the inside.

ROSC came from that. Anonymous, standardized data — built by clinicians, owned by clinicians, used to make the next job better than the last. This isn't wellness. It's accountability.

For the ones who keep going. The ones who know burnout is real — and know the system is the cause, not the worker.

No hype. No fluff. Just the truth, measured.
